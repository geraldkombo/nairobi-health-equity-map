# Democratizing Data: The Kenya Health Equity Map for Community-Led Monitoring

## Abstract Submission — 3rd CSS Knowledge Dissemination Forum
## Sub-theme 2: Digital Health and Evidence Generation Through Community-Led Monitoring

As Kenya transitions toward integrated Universal Health Coverage (UHC), the risk of marginalized communities being excluded from formal health systems grows. Deep geographical and socioeconomic disparities continue to limit access to essential services. The Kenya Health Equity Map is an innovative, fully offline-capable digital tool designed to support Community-Led Monitoring (CLM) by translating complex health, demographic, and infrastructure data into plain, accessible visual evidence for local advocates.

Currently, national health statistics frequently mask severe county-level realities. While national discourse focuses on broad systemic improvements, our tool visualizes stark contrasts: Nairobi County experiences the lowest health inequity (scoring 40 on our Priority Gravity Score), while Turkana County faces the highest burden (scoring 92). The lived reality in marginalized counties remains alarming. Tana River faces a 72.5% poverty rate, while home birth rates remain critically high in Mandera (50%), Tana River (48%), and Turkana (47%). Nationwide, maternal and child health indicators still show significant delays, with only 29% of women attending their first antenatal visit during the critical first trimester.

The map calculates a simple, transparent health equity score based on community vulnerabilities, population size, and physical travel time to health facilities. By mapping 1,699 community-verified health facilities, the tool highlights severe infrastructure gaps as hard evidence. For instance, in Elgeyo-Marakwet, the current validated baseline reveals only 1 facility serving an estimated 454,000 people.

Designed specifically to combat digital exclusion, the tool functions entirely offline without requiring app store installations or logins. By providing a clear visual narrative of local health gaps, the Kenya Health Equity Map empowers communities to generate actionable evidence, hold County Health Management Teams (CHMTs) accountable, and forcefully advocate for targeted resource allocation.
