Democratizing Evidence Generation: An Offline-First Civic Intelligence Platform for Community-Led Monitoring of Health Equity in Kenya

**Body of the abstract (must not exceed 300 words):**

**Introduction:** CLM needs evidence communities can access, understand, and act on. Kenya's 47 counties span an inequity gap — a woman in Turkana is 52 points worse off on the PGS than a woman in Nairobi. Yet the data remains scattered across national averages that obscure local crises. Without spatial evidence, communities advocate without a quantified baseline.

**Description of intervention:** The Kenya Health Equity Map is an offline-first platform that translates health infrastructure data into actionable evidence. It uses the Priority Gap Score — a simple 0–100 measure combining travel time to the nearest clinic, poverty rate, and population pressure on facilities, all published as a verifiable paper calculation in plain language. Users explore an interactive map, generate printable county briefs for CHMT meetings, and compare any two counties side-by-side. It requires no login, no installation, collects no data, and works offline after one load — designed for the communities CLM serves.

**Findings and lessons learned:** The PGS reveals systemic disparities: Nairobi scores 40 (618 facilities, 16.5% poverty) versus Turkana scoring 92 (47% home births, 42.6% hardcore poverty — the highest nationally). It maps 1,699 community-validated facilities and shows that complex access metrics translate into simple advocacy tools. Community teams in Turkana, Tana River (72.5% poverty, PGS 89), and Elgeyo-Marakwet validated the data and contributed facilities to OpenStreetMap — expanding the evidence base communities themselves own.

**Conclusion and next steps:** By equipping communities with printable evidence and a transparent, trackable score, it strengthens CLM outcomes beyond process reporting. A community can now show CHMT planners: "Turkana scored 92 last year — has resource allocation closed this gap?" It operates at zero ongoing cost, independently of donor cycles, freely available for any community to use or adapt. All code uses a license that locks no community out.
