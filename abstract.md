# Democratizing Data: The Kenya Health Equity Map for Community-Led Monitoring

## Abstract Submission — 3rd CSS Knowledge Dissemination Forum
## Sub-theme 2: Digital Health and Evidence Generation Through Community-Led Monitoring

As Kenya transitions toward integrated Universal Health Coverage (UHC) and domestic health financing, the risk of marginalized communities being excluded from formal health systems grows. Deep geographical and socioeconomic disparities continue to limit access to specialized healthcare and essential services. The Kenya Health Equity Map is an innovative, fully offline-capable digital tool designed to support Community-Led Monitoring (CLM) by translating complex health, demographic, and infrastructure data into plain, accessible visual evidence for local advocates.

Currently, national health statistics frequently mask severe county-level realities. While national discourse focuses on broad systemic improvements, our data visualizes stark contrasts: Nairobi County experiences the lowest health inequity (scoring 40 on our Priority Gravity Score), while Turkana County faces the highest burden (scoring 92). The lived reality in marginalized counties remains alarming; for example, Tana River faces a 72.5% poverty rate, and home birth rates remain critically high in Mandera (50%), Tana River (48%), and Turkana (47%). Nationwide, maternal and child health indicators still show significant delays, with only 29% of women attending their first antenatal visit during the critical first trimester, and neonatal mortality stagnating at 21 deaths per 1,000 live births.

The map calculates a simple health equity score based on community vulnerabilities, population size, and physical travel time to health facilities. By mapping 1,699 community-verified health facilities (representing approximately 10% of Kenya's 17,400+ registered facilities), the tool highlights severe infrastructure gaps. For instance, in Elgeyo-Marakwet, the current validated map reveals only 1 facility serving an estimated 454,000 people within the sample baseline.

Designed entirely for low-resource settings, the tool functions without internet access, ensuring that rural community health workers and advocates are not excluded by the digital divide. By providing a clear, color-coded visual narrative of local health gaps, the Kenya Health Equity Map empowers communities to generate actionable evidence, hold health systems accountable, and forcefully advocate for equitable resource allocation.
