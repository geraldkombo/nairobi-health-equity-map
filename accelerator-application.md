## 2. Problem Statement

Africa's health systems generate vast quantities of data — censuses, household surveys, facility registries, DHIS2 reports — but that data is scattered across government portals, locked in technical formats, and aggregated into national averages that mask severe local inequities.

A Ministry of Health official sees "88% facility delivery rate nationally." A community in Turkana sees 47% home births. Both are looking at the same country. The national number is true. The local number is also true. The gap between them is where communities live and die.

**The core problem:** Health data exists but is inaccessible to the people who need it most — community health promoters, peer networks, and local health committees who make decisions about resource allocation, budget priorities, and advocacy targets.

---

## 3. Solution

EquityMap is an offline-first digital platform that translates complex public health data into a visual, actionable evidence base for community-led health advocacy.

**Core product:** The Priority Gap Score (PGS) — a transparent 0-100 composite that combines travel time to clinic, poverty rate, and population-per-facility for every county. Higher score = greater inequity.

**Key features:**
- **County Brief (printable):** One-page PDF with PGS, indicator breakdown, and source citations — designed as a formal planning document for County Health Management Team meetings
- **Side-by-Side Comparison:** Compare any two counties across all indicators with dynamic advocacy takeaways
- **Offline Mode:** Full functionality after one load. No internet needed. No installation. No login.
- **Zero surveillance:** No cookies, no tracking, no personal data collected. No device permissions requested.

## 8. Key Application Responses

### Why EquityMap?

> "Most health-tech tools focus on service delivery — tracking vaccinations, logging household data, reporting stockouts. EquityMap solves a different problem: it makes infrastructure inequity visible. Before you can fix a broken system, you need to see where the gaps are. We give communities that vision."

### What will the seed funding be used for?

1. **Field validation (40%):** Deploy in 3 Amref-priority ASAL counties, train 30 CBOs in using PGS for budget advocacy, measure PGS change over 12 months
2. **Product expansion (35%):** Add ward-level granularity, integrate DHIS2 data pipeline, build CHMT dashboard for real-time equity monitoring
3. **Team (25%):** Community partnerships manager + part-time geospatial data engineer

### Pan-African scaling potential

> "The methodology is country-agnostic. Any country with a census, a poverty survey, and a health facility list can generate a PGS. We've designed the data pipeline to be reproducible — a developer with basic skills can rebuild the complete dataset when new survey data is published. We're starting with Kenya because the data is available and the CLM infrastructure (Amref, eCHIS, iMonitor) is mature. Next: Nigeria (36 states + FCT), Uganda, Ethiopia, Tanzania."
