# From Data to Demand: A Community-Owned Digital Evidence Platform for Health Equity Monitoring in Kenya

*Gerald Kombo - Independent Researcher and Developer, Kenya Health Equity Map*

## Sub-theme 2: Digital Health and Evidence Generation Through Community-Led Monitoring

**Introduction:** Community-led monitoring depends on evidence that citizens can understand and act on. National statistics often mask severe county-level disparities, and when data remains locked in technical reports, communities lack the proof needed to hold health managers accountable.

**Description of intervention:** The Kenya Health Equity Map is a free digital platform that turns complex demographic data into a transparent Priority Gap Score (0-100). No login, no data collection, and no account are required. A Community Health Promoter can open it on a basic smartphone, tap their county, and view travel times, poverty rates, and facility crowding in a single place.

**Findings and lessons learned:** Turkana scores 92 out of 100. A mother there travels 90 minutes to reach a clinic, 47% of births happen at home, and 42.6% of households live in poverty. Nairobi scores 40, with 618 mapped facilities and 16.5% poverty. This 52-point gap means a woman in Turkana travels 90 minutes for basic care while a Nairobi resident accesses a clinic within minutes. Communities in Turkana, Tana River (score 89, 72.5% poverty), and Elgeyo-Marakwet validated these findings against local knowledge and independently added missing clinics to OpenStreetMap.

**Conclusion and next steps:** The platform operates at zero ongoing cost and is independent of any single organization. Communities can load the evidence on a phone, attend legally mandated County Health Management Team meetings, and ask directly: "Last year, mothers in Turkana travelled 90 minutes to reach a clinic and nearly half of births happened at home. What has changed since then?"
