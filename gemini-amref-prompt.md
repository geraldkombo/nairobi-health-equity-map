**Description of intervention:** The Kenya Health Equity Map turns public data into a simple score anyone can verify with pen and paper. The Priority Gap Score (PGS) combines three factors: travel time to the nearest clinic, poverty rate, and population per facility. The platform works fully offline on a basic smartphone after one load. No login, no data collection, no installation. **Findings and lessons learned:** Turkana scores 92 on the Priority Gap Scale where 0 is best and 100 is worst (42.6% poverty, 47% home births). Nairobi scores 40 (16.5% poverty, 618 facilities). **Conclusion and next steps:** A community can now bring a printed brief to a County Health Management Team meeting and ask: "Turkana scored 92 last year. Has resource allocation closed this gap?"
